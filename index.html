The Tribunals Reforms (Rationalisation and Conditions of Service) Ordinance, 2021
 
The Central government has amended 10 laws to remove several appellate bodies through an ordinance – the Tribunals Reforms (Rationalisation and Conditions of Service) Ordinance, 2021 – which was notified on April 4.

The laws amended include the Cinematograph Act, 1952; Copyright Act, 1957; Customs Act, 1962; Patents Act, 1970; Airport Authority of India Act, 1994; Trade Marks Act, 1999; Geographical Indications of Goods (Registration and Protection) Act, 1999; Protection of Plant Varieties and Farmers’ Rights Act, 2001; Control of National Highways (Land and Traffic) Act, 2002; and the Finance Act, 2017.

SC - Sec.438, Cr.P.C. clearly relates to the grant of Anticipatory Bail by the Court

Therefore, such an order must be a reasoned one. — Hon’ble Justices N.V. Ramana, Surya Kant and Aniruddha Bose [28-05-2021]
